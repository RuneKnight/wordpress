Economic Activity: A Slowing Ascent

The Coincident Composite Index, a key barometer of the current economic state, indicates that the economy continues to expand, though the pace of recovery is moderating. Strong export performance is counteracted by sluggish domestic demand.

Consumption: Treading Water

Consumer spending is stagnant, weighed down by high interest rates and persistent inflation. While service consumption shows some resilience, goods consumption, especially for durables, has declined, as reflected in the retail sales data.

Investment: A Tale of Two Sectors

The investment landscape is divided. Equipment investment is showing signs of recovery, led by the semiconductor sector. Conversely, construction investment continues to contract due to a downturn in the real estate market.

Labor Market: Stable but Stratified

The labor market remains relatively healthy with a low unemployment rate. However, job growth is concentrated in the service sector and among older age groups, while manufacturing employment continues to lag.

Prices: Inflation Cools Down

The overall inflation rate has eased, approaching the central bank's target. Core inflation, which excludes volatile food and energy prices, has also shown a downward trend, suggesting that underlying price pressures are weakening.

External Trade: Exports Lead the Way

Exports continue their strong growth trajectory, significantly contributing to overall economic performance. The trade surplus is widening as export growth outpaces that of imports, which have seen a more moderate recovery.
